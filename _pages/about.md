<p style="font-size: 1.3em; font-weight: 500; color: #333; border-left: 4px solid #52adc8; padding-left: 12px; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
I teach AI to ask <em>why</em>, not just <em>what</em>.
</p>

I'm a third-year PhD student at [Shanghai Jiao Tong University](https://www.sjtu.edu.cn/), working at the intersection of **causal reasoning** and **large language models**. I am co-supervised by [Prof. Yu Qiao](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=gFtI-8QAAAAJ) and [Dr. Chaochao Lu](https://causallu.com/), with affiliations at both SJTU and [Shanghai AI Lab](https://www.shlab.org.cn/).

My research pursues a single thread: equipping AI systems with the ability to reason about *cause and effect*, not just pattern-match on correlations. This spans:
- **Causal Evaluation** — benchmarking whether LLMs/VLMs truly understand causal relationships ([CELLO](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.19131), [CaLM](https://opencausalab.github.io/CaLM/))
- **Causal Discovery** — scaling neural methods to discover causal structures from data ([CauScale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.02449), [CauScientist](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.02449))
- **Personalization & Generation** — building models that adapt to users and generate conditioned on causal structure ([IP-Dialog](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.02449), [ConditionVideo](https://pengbo807.github.io/conditionvideo-website/))

Feel free to reach out if you'd like to collaborate or just chat about causality and AI.
